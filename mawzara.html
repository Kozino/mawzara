The Problem

A blind spot at the heart of national resilience.

The 2017 blockade exposed what insiders already knew: Qatar's food supply chain was invisible. That gap remains open in 2026.

01

No live shipment visibility

The 2–6 week window from port of origin to Hamad Port is completely blind. Ministries and importers find out about delays when ships don't arrive.

02

Reserve tracking is manual

Days-of-cover calculations are done in spreadsheets. No automated alerts when a food category drops below threshold. TASMU tracks what's landed — not what's coming.

03

Supplier concentration untracked

78% of Qatar's rice comes from a single country. No platform flags this. The 2017 blockade happened partly because these blind spots went undetected.

"We find out about delays when the vessel doesn't show up."

— Food importer, Doha (user interview)

The Solution

One platform.
Complete visibility.

A bilingual Arabic / English SaaS platform built for two users — the Ministry of Commerce and private importers — with one shared goal: knowing what's coming before it arrives.

Ministry (MOCI) Food Importers Arabic RTL First

Live shipment tracking

Port-to-warehouse visibility via Maersk, MSC, and CMA CGM APIs. Real-time vessel position and ETA updates.

Reserve dashboard

Days-of-cover by food category with automated SMS + email threshold alerts for MOCI and importers.

Supplier risk scoring

Concentration flags, geopolitical risk index, and diversification scoring. Know before the news does.

Automated alerts

Policy-level alerts for MOCI, operational delay alerts for importers — no spreadsheets, no surprises.
